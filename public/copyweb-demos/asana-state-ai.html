02 / CHAPTERS

Break the long read into moments people can remember.

A sticky chapter rail and repeated visual modules make the report feel guided instead of heavy. This is suitable for IGNAI event recaps, community research, and AI trend observation pages.

Illustration first

Use playful abstract diagrams, community photos, or generated editorial art to introduce each insight before the numbers arrive.

Data second

Keep statistics large, sparse, and human-readable. Let each number do one job instead of filling the screen with dashboards.

Chapter anchors

Readers can jump between Introduction, Signals, Practice, Cases, and Takeaways without losing context.

Scroll rhythm

Alternate quiet text sections with colorful cards so the page keeps energy across a long narrative.

03 / TAKEAWAYS

Turn a report into a community operating artifact.

1

Collect field notes

Gather member stories, event quotes, and tool workflows before designing the final report.

2

Map evidence to chapters

Each chapter should combine a human story, one data point, and a recommended practice.

3

Publish as a reusable format

The same structure can support monthly AI trend reports, event recaps, and sponsor-ready community briefings.

Community reports can feel like products.
